Welcome, Guests of Ar-Rahman.

Cave of Hira is at the top of Jabal al-Nour or Mountain of Light northeast of Mecca. This mountain is 634 meters high. The cave is really narrow, only allowing three persons to sit inside it at one time. Before his prophetic mission began, our great Prophet Muhammad would go into this cave for retreats where he spent many nights particularly in the month of Ramadan and contemplated the mysteries of Allah's creations in the heavens and the earth, far away from people. He was nature-inclined to solitude.

This spot enjoys a particularity that is highlighted on several grounds but most importantly because:

That the cave is secluded and far away from the reach of noise and people's fuss, as its ascent to the mountain was not easy due to the bad route and its ruggedness. The distance from the Kaaba to the cave is roughly 4 km. There was also a crack in its west side through which he could visualize the Kaaba during this quieting-of-self process. It was in this desolate and sterile spot, on the top of this mountain, that the mission of the Prophet started. The first verses of the Quran were revealed to him here: (Read in the name of your Lord who created). Terrified by the great experience, the Prophet -peace and blessings be upon him- retreated to his wife Khadijah may Allah be pleased with her- trembling and fearful, saying: «Cover me! Cover me! Wrap me up!». May the blessings and peace of Allah be upon him, his family, and his companions until the Day of Judgment.

Dear Muslim brother, Dear Muslim sister:
(Visitors of Hira Cave)

Learning the biography of Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) is essential for each and every Muslim; for taking him as an example in our lives will elevate us to higher levels of living. However, some of the practices observed by some visitors—may Allah guide us and them—are not from Islam. These include rubbing stones, seeking blessings from trees, kissing objects, or other inappropriate acts that run in conflict with what the Prophet brought. Let us follow his noble example and avoid practices that deviate from his teachings. Is this anything that was done by the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—or ordered by him, or practiced by his noble companions? Never, never. Worst of all these actions, however, is the major sin of seeking aid or intercession from other than Allah, the Almighty. This hadith highlights the very point that the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, expressed when he counseled Ibn Abbas, may Allah be pleased with them both, with the following words: «Young man, be mindful of Allah, and He Will protect you. Be mindful of Allah, and you will find Him before you. If you ask, ask Allah. If you seek help, seek help from Allah....»

For this reason, it is our duty to give sincere advice and denounce such actions, as the Prophet—peace and blessings be upon him—said: «Whoever among you sees an evil, let him change it with his hand. If he is unable, then with his tongue. And if he is unable, then with his heart—and that is the weakest of faith.» Narrated by Muslim.

This is part of the rights of a Muslim upon another Muslim and a fulfillment of Allah's command in Surah Al-Asr: (Except for those who have believed and done righteous deeds and advised each other to truth and advised each other to patience.)

Some people visit the Cave of Hira during their Hajj or Umrah or at some other times and undergo much hardship and expend a lot of effort due to their wrongful notion that by this action, they attain nearness with Allah. Yet, this is not the case. Such an effort not only carries no reward and nearness to Allah, but it even falls into that which Allah has forbidden. Others commit newly invented acts—innovations—that undermine the completeness of faith. Some visitors—may Allah guide them—perform practices that run contrary to the message of monotheism brought by the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him.

The Prophet, peace be upon him, used to worship Allah in the Cave of Hira before the descent of the revelation. When the Quran started to be revealed to him, he never again went to the cave for prayer, supplication, or any other act of worship. The Prophet lived in Mecca for approximately 13 years after receiving revelation, and there is no evidence that he ever visited Hira or any other places people mistakenly think are recommended or virtuous to visit.

The only place in Mecca that is prescribed for worship, prayer, and supplication is the Masjid al-Haram alone. With regard to the places visited by the pilgrims, Arafah, Mina, and Muzdalifah should only be visited during the days of Hajj. It is not permissible to visit them before or after that time, and it is likewise not permissible to move the rituals to an earlier or later period than what was specified in the Sharia. The only place meant for regular worship in Makkah is Masjid al-Haram, the Sacred Mosque, and a person may enter it and offer prayers therein at any time, night or day. This is asserted in the hadith: «O Banu Abd Manaf, do not prevent anyone from performing Tawaf around this House and from praying here at whatever hour of night or day.» Reported by the authors of the Sunan.

Dear Muslim, if these three rituals of standing at Arafah, staying in Muzdalifah, and stoning the Jamarat were prescribed by Allah to His servants since the time of Ibrahim—peace be upon him—and some say since the time of Adam—peace be upon him—and were restricted to certain times and conditions, how then could people invent other places to visit for worship and claim closeness to Allah? For example, some visit the Cave of Hira or other sites, assigning them significance and allowing worship there at any time. This contradicts the teachings of Islam and the practices of the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him), who did not prescribe such actions. Worship must adhere to the guidance revealed by Allah and practiced by His Messenger, without introducing innovations or acts not sanctioned in the religion. After the migration of Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) to Medina, he came back many times to Mecca. However, he never visited the Cave of Hira. If visiting it were an act of worship or a means of drawing closer to Allah, the Prophet—peace be upon him—would have been the first to do so. Indeed, he used to worship there before his prophethood, and it is in that cave that he received the divine revelation—a unique moment that never happened to anyone before or after him. Also, it is well documented that during his Umrah as an act of performance of the Farewell Pilgrimage, a huge number of Muslims accompanied him—nearly all of those who could join him. Yet neither he nor any of his Companions ever climbed Cave Hira', ever visited it, or sought blessings at any of the sites around Makkah. And after his right-guided caliphs and others of the early generations, they did not travel to Hira Cave or similar places for prayer, supplication, or seeking blessings. If this had been a prescribed and recommended act that Allah rewards, the Prophet would have been the most knowledgeable of it and the most eager for it. Since they did not do any of that, it is known to be an introduced innovation forbidden in Sharia and religion. Whoever disobeys after that and turns it into worship, closeness, and obedience, has followed a path other than theirs, and has legislated in religion what Allah did not permit. He, the Almighty, said: (Indeed, in the Messenger of Allah you have an excellent example for whoever has hope in Allah and the Last Day and remembers Allah often.)
